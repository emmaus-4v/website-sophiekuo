Amara/the Darkness

"We came into existence together. We are the same. (...) Chuck and I, we're twins. Creation and destruction, light and dark, balance. And when we split apart, all this was created."
- Amara

The Darkness, or Amara is one of the two most powerful beings in the universe, together with her brother, God. She is the main anatagonist of season 11.

First she just wants revenge on God (Chuck) for locking her up at the beginning of time. Eventually she reconciles with him and they dissapear together at the end of the season. She returns in season 15, where God asks her for help starting over, destroying all universes and making new ones, but she refuses. She initially doesn't take a side, but later she takes the Winchester's because she likes the earth. However God and Darkness can't exist without each other, because the universe would be inbalanced, so Dean wants to kill her too. After she discovers this, she let's God absorb her and her power. After Jack absorbs God's power, he says he and Amara are living in harmony.

Azazel

"Azazel... was a fanatic. A true believer in Lucifer's "master plan"."
- Ramiel

Azazel is a Prince of Hell, a powerful demon that serves Lucifer. He was the main antagonist in seasons 1 and 2.

Ten years after Mary Winchester gives him permisson to take Sam to save John, he walks into little Sammy's nursery and bleeds in his mouth, which gives him powers. Mary walks in and he kills her. He makes other "special children" like Sam, how many is not clear. To find the fittest, he grabs a few children at a time and puts them in a ghost town until they kill each other. A man named Jake wins and Azazel uses him to get to the Devil's Gate, where he can't get because it's in a giant devil's trap. He opens the gate, but then the Winchesters appear and Dean kills Azazel with the Colt.

Billie

"It's you, Dean. It's always been you. Death-defying. Rule-breaking. You are everything I lived to set right. To put down. To tame. You are human disorder incarnate."
- Billie to Dean

Billie was formerly a reaper, but became Death after the old Death died and she died too. She is the secondary anatagonist of season 15.

Her goal at first was to make sure the Winchesters didn't come back to life again when they died. The old Death had apparantley found it funny, but since he died Billie was the reaper in charge. When the brothers are caught by the FBI and put in solitary, they basically go insane, so they make a deal with Billie to get them out, but in return one of them has to die. When Castiel hears about this, he kills Billie so no Winchester has to die. After this she becomes Death, because the first reaper to die after Death is killed becomes Death. In season 15, she helps the Winchesters kill God by bringing back Jack and giving them a plan, but it turns out she only did because she wanted to take over herself. Team Free Will 2.0 turns on her, and Dean and Castiel go to kill her. She chases them through the bunker and traps them, when she finally bursts in, the Empty is there, summoned by Cas' love confession and his true happiness, and it takes Billie.

Gabriel

"All I did on Earth was run. I'm not running anymore."
- Gabriel

Gabriel was first thought to be a trickster, but turned out to be an archangel. He helped the brothers on numerous occasions.

He appears first in season 2, tormenting the brothers by sticking Sam in a time loop and making some funny things happen at an university. When he sticks the brothers in a television world where they have to play their roles, just like they have to play their roles in real life as Michael's and Lucifer's vessels, they discover he is Gabriel, the archangel. Later, when Sam and Dean are captured by pagan Gods, Gabriel goes to save them, and when Lucifer shows up, Gabriel gets seemingly killed by him. Later it is revealed Lucifer merely killed a clone, the real gabriel went to pagan god Loki and his seasons and they helped him hide, but after a while they sold him, because they blamed him for Odin's death at Lucifer's hands. Gabriel was with Prince of hell Asmodeus, who tortured him and milked his grace. When Asmodeus kidnaps a human, Arthur Ketch, and he sees Gabriel he decides to rescue Gabriel and flee to the Winchesters. Gabriel goes with the others to the Apocalypse world, where he stays behind to buy the boys time and gets killed by Alternate Michael.

God/Chuck

"Those other toys, they don't spark joy. But Sam and Dean, the real Sam and Dean, they do. They challenge me... they disappoint me... they surprise me. They're the ones. "
- God

God, or Chuck was first thought to be a prophet who had dreams baout the Winchesters, but later he revealed to be the creator of the universe. He is the main antagonist of season 15.

The brothers and Castiel first meet Chuck because he wrote a book series based on his dreams about them: the Supernatural books. They go to his house and Castiel says that he is a prophet. He helps the Winchesters several times, but eventually they assume he is dead because Kevin became a prophet and there can only be one prophet at the same time. He reappeared in season 11 to the brothers, to stop his sister, the Darkness. He is at first shown to be lovable, if not a bit disconnected from the beings he created. He doesn't defeat Amara but instead makes up with her and they dissapear. He appears again at the end of season 14, concerned about Jack's enormous power, and tells the brothers and Cas to kill him. They go to do that, but then Sam notices Chuck is enjoying this, he calls him out, so Chuck kills Jack himself and plunges them into chaos before dissapearing. His goal in season 15 is to start over, create new universes and discard the old ones, but he is not at full power, because Sam shot him with the Equalizer, which was originally for killing Jack. But even after the wound heals, he still wants to beat the Winchesters. He thinks he has it all in hand, because his son Michael turned on the boys, but turns out they lied to Michael. Jack had been absorbing power from Lucifer and Michael, and then he drained Chuck's power too, leaving him a simple human.

John Winchester

"You know, when you were a kid, I'd come home from a hunt, and after what I'd seen, I'd be, I'd be wrecked. And you, you'd come up to me and you, you'd put your hand on my shoulder and you'd look me in the eye and you'd... You'd say "It's okay, Dad". [...] You shouldn't have had to say that to me, I should have been saying that to you. You know, I put, I put too much on your shoulders, I made you grow up too fast. You took care of Sammy, you took care of me. You did that, and you didn't complain, not once. I just want you to know that I am so proud of you. "
- John to Dean

John Winchester is the brothers' father.

After Mary's death John is obsessed with killing Azazel, who killed her, and he raises their two sons in the hunting life. When John went missing, 26 year old Dean went to his brother in law school and they started hunting together again. Eventually his sons found him, he was finding the Colt to kill Azazel. But after an encounter with the demon they get into a car crash and land in the hospital. Here John makes a deal to save Dean for his own life and the Colt. He went to Hell, but escaped when the Devil's Gate opened and he distracted Azazel long enough for Dean to kill him, before ascending to heaven.
