Amara/the Darkness

We came into existence together. We are the same. (...) Chuck and I, we're twins. Creation and destruction, light and dark, balance. And when we split apart, all this was created.
- Amara

The Darkness, or Amara is one of the two most powerful beings in the universe, together with her brother, God. She is the main anatagonist of season 11.

First she just wants revenge on God (Chuck) for locking her up at the beginning of time. Eventually she reconciles with him and they dissapear together at the end of the season. She returns in season 15, where God asks her for help starting over, destroying all universes and making new ones, but she refuses. She initially doesn't take a side, but later she takes the Winchester's because she likes the earth. However God and Darkness can't exist without each other, because the universe would be inbalanced, so Dean wants to kill her too. After she discovers this, she let's God absorb her and her power. After Jack absorbs God's power, he says he and Amara are living in harmony.

Azazel

"Azazel... was a fanatic. A true believer in Lucifer's "master plan"."
- Ramiel

Azazel is a Prince of Hell, a powerful demon that serves Lucifer. He was the main antagonist in seasons 1 and 2.

Ten years after Mary Winchester gives him permisson to take Sam to save John, he walks into little Sammy's nursery and bleeds in his mouth, which gives him powers. Mary walks in and he kills her. He makes other "special children" like Sam, how many is not clear. To find the fittest, he grabs a few children at a time and puts them in a ghost town until they kill each other. A man named Jake wins and Azazel uses him to get to the Devil's Gate, where he can't get because it's in a giant devil's trap. He opens the gate, but then the Winchesters appear and Dean kills Azazel with the Colt.

Billie

It's you, Dean. It's always been you. Death-defying. Rule-breaking. You are everything I lived to set right. To put down. To tame. You are human disorder incarnate.
- Billie to Dean

Billie was formerly a reaper, but became Death after the old Death died and she died too. She is the secondary anatagonist of season 15.

Her goal at first was to make sure the Winchesters didn't come back to life again when they died. The old Death had apparantley found it funny, but since he died Billie was the reaper in charge. When the brothers are caught by the FBI and put in solitary, they basically go insane, so they make a deal with Billie to get them out, but in return one of them has to die. When Castiel hears about this, he kills Billie so no Winchester has to die. After this she becomes Death, because the first reaper to die after Death is killed becomes Death. In season 15, she helps the Winchesters kill God by bringing back Jack and giving them a plan, but it turns out she only did because she wanted to take over herself. Team Free Will 2.0 turns on her, and Dean and Castiel go to kill her. She chases them through the bunker and traps them, when she finally bursts in, the Empty is there, summoned by Cas' love confession and his true happiness, and it takes Billie.

Gabriel

It's you, Dean. It's always been you. Death-defying. Rule-breaking. You are everything I lived to set right. To put down. To tame. You are human disorder incarnate.
- Billie

Gabriel was first thought to be a trickster, but turned out to be an archangel. He helped the brothers on numerous occasions.

He appears first in season 2, tormenting the brothers by sticking Sam in a time loop and making some funny things happen at an university. When he sticks the brothers in a television world where they have to play their roles, just like they have to play their roles in real life as Michael's and Lucifer's vessels, they discover he is Gabriel, the archangel. Later, when Sam and Dean are captured by pagan Gods, Gabriel goes to save them, and when Lucifer shows up, Gabriel gets killed by him.
